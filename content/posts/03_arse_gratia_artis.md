*The aims of art are incommensurate (as the mathematicians say) with social aims. The aim of an artist is not to solve a problem irrefutably, but to make people love life in all its countless, inexhaustible manifestations.* 
(Leo Tolstoy)

What is art? [Term of endearment] don't quote me, don’t quoth me, ever more. 
But certainly not whatever follows.

—

Space being infinite, does not give a damn. Nearly five thousand words of utter and complete balderdash (understatement), and this was the one sentence I chose to omit[^1], on the grounds of a comma whose presence would break the scheme, whose absence would leave its necessity under-implied, and whose parenthesisation was out of the question (aesthetic reasons, inter alia). Yay, Oxford. (The subjunctive was subjectively judged unnecessary.) The hypocrisy of not having charted the Olympians off to a similar fate has not escaped me. So a guised Apollo geysing (American pronunciation, please, just this once) up towers of steam in wrath, (however so briefly) dropping the lyre to string the bow once more, propping the liar to rethink the roles of one’s roar[^2]; giving Æsclepius a taste of his own creation - all justified, and in part, anticipated. For far more than this obstreperous obsession obliteratively obliviating the realisation which would otherwise have obviously obviated the need for any vacillations between form and function[^3]. (I’ll try not to do that too often. No promises.) 

The laziness evident (not deprecating my love for pater-jokes) from the fact that I’m reusing a phrase for the title – and that I know what it’s going to be, apparently (you’ll see a strikethrough if this gets retconned) – might already have indicated that this isn’t the blog you were most likely expecting it to not be. I will double down on negatives no longer. Hereon. This is, indeed, owing to the fact that I have nothing to say, or, more precisely, the nothing I have to say is more obvious than usual, an interlude, an anthological set of unwanted ads, an interim, an entracte in which one gets a drink, discusses the possibility of the supporting characters having better voices than the lead, wonders if this notion comes from a less or more biased perspective given the O in VOSTF stands for Italian – but not the one one is familiar with, certainly not in the register one is comfortable with – then gets another drink to make the remainder of the wait for a proverbially fat lady to put one out of this misery slightly bearable (yeah, okay, call me uncultured, can't keep this folly of attributing the fact that I’m the least cultured person I know to sampling bias going any longer). From the way that last sentence went, this is more likely going to end inevitably up like a feline attempt at reinstating a ball of yarn to its original configuration - expect far more loose ends than earlier, I say, finally. A Tom, clearly, but could not be further from a Swiftie. It’s easy to see a Jerry-meandering on slippery slopes from afar, but hard to decipher the eponymous adornments on the collar of a Spike? Two Shoes retains the cupidity of trading goodies for face value. Footnotes are, I feel, necessary for the indispensable purpose of keeping such side-thoughts from confounding you further right away[^4]. How else would one ensure that your bewilderment (if any) is strictly related to my characterisation of what I have in mind (the existence of which remains for the moment purely conjectural) for this text and not the references (to my all time favourite show, which happens also to be the first place I remember seeing the unmodified version of the title, hence a roaring Leo – though in all honesty just lucked out there – ‘neath the banner, couldn’t keep them in any longer) that followed?

Right, right, off the bat – or the racquet, the paddle, the stick, or any other pertinent piece of equipment capable of transporting balls, feder or otherwise, across fields, over nets, beyond boundaries, through easily abstracted, but mostly elastic, collisions – this is a non-zero-sum game, (Adv. Game Theory was far from my favourite, so let’s say I’m quoting Renner’s character from that atypically decent adaptation of The Story of Your Life, can’t use the same adjective for Villeneuve’s more recent ambitions of course) and one in which I am the one at a disadvantage, in terms of all the parameters beginning with qua- and seemingly relevant given the context that you can think of (quadruped-inclusiveness, unsurprisingly, qualifies; and that definitely sounds like a reference - in fact, this is all just references, nothing else, the ones you get are the ones you get, the ones you don’t are the ones to Star Trek).  Truth be told, (universal cue to prepare for lies; this guy will say just about anything to justify the stupidest play on words) that step into the shadows, was, is, and will remain for some time, the status quo. I was prepped to sound the klaxon, call all anchors aweigh, Caesar-ly spurn the self like fawning curs (away)[^5] - but anchors have a weird way of unexpectedly arresting momentum by sheer inertia. I find to my ever-increasing horror that some layer of my internal model of external mechanics still runs on Aristotelian physics. There was this game on some old Nokia phones (or was it Motorola? Anyway, not as good as Snakes 3D, but then nothing will ever be able to match the brilliance of those thirty-six chambers which gave my thumbs more dexterity than Shaolin ever could have) that required the player to time the release of one floor from some pendulum (let's just buy this premise) onto the rest of a sky-scraper and make it go as high as possible without collapsing due to improper stacking. One of those instances where you want the collisions to be entirely plastic, I guess. The game was obviously designed to brainwash people like me, and it worked. So my oscillating mind keeps confounding these incoherencies for nuggets and drops them haphazardly down one atop the other, in blatant ignorance of the fact that their tangential origins will undoubtedly make them need to cling to some super-bly scripted number for support at the bottom of this absolute lack of a structure[^6]. Swinging back to anchors (though maybe further in the opposite direction than necessary) - I have had but a precious few to keep me tethered to the ever evolving congenitors, critics, and consumers of (pop) culture (Rube Goldberg’s humanity; and this too can mean two things, can mean three, but does at most one - more on this later perhaps) over time. (This particular ripple of consciousness has already lasted for longer than the length of an Instagram reel, but I can’t see the light of a line break at the end of this tunnel just yet. Treating every sentence like a paragraph, every clause – guilty more than ever of committing gross insubordinations, yelling “screw ye!” to ‘em dashes, to these commas, to all separators trying in vain to shove it down some unspoken, but still expected, hierarchy – like a sentence, and every word like the unnecessary fixture it is, might help.) Lowering these anchors then, letting them down further and further, very close to the sea bed now; Mephistopheles is just beneath, reaching up to grab me (customary allusion; check) but (a -120 degree turn made possible by two lightning-fast clicks of the right thumb, a dash to the finish imminent) I am saved from that final embrace by a tug on the hawser, by assertions of red, blue, green; by a mixing of sources and not of pigments, not of just reflectors (you can’t possibly be reading this), by the existence of depths easily denied but not quite trivially cached, by the electrolytic trickery (feature, not bug) of mere patterns generating potentials between odes; this battery of sans-serif a ladder of blue in a sea of white (very hard on the eyes to climb to be fair, but makes sense). I tried my damned hardest (hyperbole) to circle back but this thought has ended quite a few parsecs from where it began, let’s just take a break. I wouldn’t read too much into any of this anyway.

The trick to not explaining things well, to be seemingly incomprehensible, is never having understood them in the first place. Keeping it safe, not hoping for the impossible, for one and one to make two - the same enervated smugness of exhausting riddles preying on statistically valid but specifically fallacious assumptions; this one is not equal to the other, an assumption on identity[^7] most fatal, tying those erstwhile vocal chords up into A Major-ly muted fugue. The trick to (poorly; also, excuse the dangle) describing something physical – having established that the abstract can otherwise only be verbalised with sublim-inal rhetorics; that while much is perceived, little is experienced – is to abstract the physical out to the extent that only an encapsulated something remains. That something is a Cone. Suspended in space, right before two receptacles, each with a plethora of cones inside (and these are the ones that betray me). All Some axes coplanar. Are we sure that it is, in fact, a Cone? Not a disk? The height’s deceptively infinitesimal at times, but certainly non-zero. Measured it myself, with mine own two lenses (exactly the ones I’m not thinking of). Its surface a mystery, a photonic funnel of radial beams, shining as if infinitely afar, rippling as the externally illuminative torch revolving around it in an eccentric orbit accelerates – making me wonder whether any beings living on this torch can, as I was promised I would, and then for a while could, but then couldn’t, and now most definitely can’t, for this numbness is quite permanent, feel the Coriolis – and confounding me into subconsciously capturing the momentary into a frame of impertinent persistence. But why a Cone, you ask, why not something Else? Simply because all Else has already slipped through the lapses of the laps esoterically taken by the Observer around it. Hence the Cone is all that remains, the unwitting subject of a soliloquy nobody (not Nobody this time, lamentably) asked for. You’re right, time to forget this - whatever this was.  Maybe the Cone will make a reappearance in some work of sciencier fiction, or spacier fantasy, but we don’t have to live in the horrifying anticipation of that moment just yet.

The (tr)icky lack of tact in making a reference, but not really, is in finding a euphemism for the reference and then a reference for this euphemism instead. Ever since that Harvey Dent of months, I have been plagued. By concerns of skewed symmetries, of reciprocities, (of the lightlike-ness of lines, of the linearity of liking light) of pitfalls and conditions of something with all the signs of a not-explicitly-acceded-to something Else. More recently, of the varied similarities of verisimilitudes, of the inescapability of a most agreeably non-optional convention, begging the question of whether one is even capable of offering anything but a fantastic lack of foresight, whether I can come even minutely close to that which was before the turntable rotated this way. Can’t put myself in shoes not my own (what if they’re smelly?), can’t think of what I would when I hardly have any idea of what I do. What do I want? Nothing much. Nothing but three wise wizards to follow this starry bedlam (sorry), conjure up a cauldron and take any duration of time, but label it two minutes, and give me the Gift of the Maggi (not sorry), the magnanimity of the magical (extra extra extra) masala, pervading every noodle, every remaining droplet of what we’ll call soup regardless of its viscosity, every slurp surrogating my slumber to a future circadian slump, accompanied, perhaps, by peas-in-our-time, along with other fruits of the soil that’d make this a mom-approved meal, even some taters if necessary, bringing the recipes of the Himalayas to this desert; oh what else could I possibly want, except perhaps something to wash this down with, if only they could in this adoration whip up a contraption of cylinders rotating on diesel for transverse contractions, some sugarcanes, a lemon, a bit of ginger, and bear me (beer me? A pint at least) that which is most veritably, most honourably, The King of the Juice(s) (okay a tad apologetic), in fact, if translated to the right amalgam of languages, quite literally, G-Juice (ah, nevermind) - that, I think, would be more than sufficient[^8] to aurumate[^9] my presently (and frankly) incensed myrrhth. (Yeah, maybe this should not be on the internet. I haven’t anonymised myself enough.)

That thing I didn’t explain earlier (can’t get more specific than this). Bit like Jack the Dripper working on a line, – whatever that is – whatever this is. (No typo there.) These words naturally have some meaning to me, but that doesn’t necessitate that they would, or should, have anything to anyone else. Such is the nature of the Wall inevitably propped up by any act of this nature, one is hardwired to consider two sides to the canvas, with the less populated one supposed to hold an unquestionable authority over the other. The Wall exists, however, to be hopefully weaker than all others, being the fruit of personal labours, so the one on the former side may try to bash a hole through with their head; finally attempt to use this skull to increase some universal utility[^10] for a change, maybe make this a board of chess with all pieces grey. All comes down to how one perceives the Wall. We don’t need any tangible ones where any addition is just another brick, we don't need [any][^11] thought control, definitely don't need a Kumar Sanu rendering the most venerable Floyd gulabi with that horrible rendition; the knowledge of this fact a curse most dismal (ignore this if your knowledge of my existence, also a curse, does not predate the pandemic, delete this from your mind if it does - this is quite possibly an info hazard). 

Now of my potentially score years and se’en, plenty will not come again; two dozen springs have borne a bore, the triad left can yield little more[^12]. Do have some ways to go yet, though, so I hand not the helm of my perambulations out that easily. Still, I keep walking, unintentionally, frown-first (a batarang traversing crimson skies); into others, into puddles, onto roads, onto the step after the last (either direction, different kinds of painful), into doors that read “pull” (in the most obvious ways possible, practically guaranteeing I’d ignore the instruction), onto little pieces of Lego (ensuring there is something to hate about them, thereby validating their right to exist); clearly shouldn’t have taken MacLean’s Navarone-ic words on peripheral vision at face value, for it clearly doesn’t work when the tunnel vision of a wandering mind sets in, with questions of why Milton and Dante both chose the same (geo)graphic direction (requisites of fiction perhaps), of whether the argument that it worked for Michelangelo permits the pervasion of de St. Exupery’s potentially perfidious persuasion on perfection or if one may persist with a personal perspective, of whether A Ginsberg’s words<sup id="fnref:42"><a href="#fn:42">42</a></sup> can finally resolve the seeming paradox, that's been troubling one's mind for ages, apparent in the popularity and diffusion of Mahmood voicing Sultanpuri’s extollations of solitude, of whether it is even possible to get out of the labyrinth that one has constructed around oneself by not particularly thinking of cardinal directions during turns of phrases. Not entirely clew-less though, there is still hope that I will find a way back to the mother-ship, if only somebody remembers to activate a beacon and transmit their coordinates, but all other senses are already party to my hallucinations so the signal must be olfactory, et voila, all I need then is in fact a Homing Bacon, the greasy kind of course, the crispy kind, the makes-you-see-the-face-of-God kind, not for a moment the français, for that is not True Bacon, and most certainly not the Francis - he must still be mad at me for having attributed to Colfer the axiom which is rightfully his.

Let’s just bring this (again, whatever this is) home as well. I did try to evade the yarn-ball prophecy but it was quite evidently impossible, travelled far and wide in hunt of the person responsible for these crimes, questioned everyone and everything about the true nature of this curse of most irregular expressions, but this Oedipus Regex (King of Tabs forever open, another title idea just flung away) was bound to reveal that I am myself the criminal I sought. Succumbed to the compulsion of waving what I had woven, of waiving what I had waved, and now the eyes that must feel attacked are not solely my own. My only respite resides in the fact that someone from the alma dealing with matters I am congenitally unworthy of opining on has already done a better job of making one realise how futile the act of thinking is. Some puns intended.
