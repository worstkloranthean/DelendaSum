Prior, Posteriori, Derogat?; or, Ego and Ed

December 6, 2025

Glass and peace alike betray proof of fragility under repeated blows. (David Mitchell)

Add this blog to that list; a glassy plaque of peevish suppositions of sheepish propositions of peace. Id est, don’t read this too many times, please.

One fears not the fall; but maybe the way it ends; with fatal foresight1. The trick, ergo, or rather the knack, as someone (whose works, perhaps, you didn’t like for the post hoc expressed fact that they were originally meant to be consumed at a modulation of frequencies much different than the current? My memory remains as dubious in servitude as ever) so beautifully postulated, is to simply miss hitting the surface so stoically threatening to refuse your continuity, a feat ostensibly achieved by developing an acute ignorance of the acceleration you’ve grown to assume the existence of blindly, if only because it’s always unflinchingly there. Another (whose works suffer your glares for having been quite frequently wrong, on account of their blinding blunders of biased sampling, but cannot be dismissed so easily as they professed an attempt to be less so instead of more of the opposite) reasoned – seemingly betraying a personal mistrust, that is not without empathisers, of the self’s motivations – that ‘twas in fact a fear not of getting hurled but of hurling oneself in a bout of mild curiosity. I’m not entirely sure which it is as the vertiginous incertitude of what lies downthread (granted, we are at a velocity quite terminal, given the symmetries of this scale) lends to my hesitation. But I’ll take my time, not like I’ve got a plan.

It’s cooking. It’s. It’s digesting. Someone’s been using my own spells recipes against me. Hath the chef sacheted (participle) and shorted the unsautéd unsorted spools of spiels of misspellings left half-baked in metafours, as they’d (lil dox-proofing theyre?) say? Splendidly; consider the batter bread and beurried, a brioche; and that is no surprise. It would be highly impertinent (the geography-aware bon mot, perhaps, is gustakhi) to underestimate the intelligence of one possibly superior in quite a few ways of the word. All the ways of words, perhaps. All the wayward wandering wondering warmongering heathenly hyperbolised hélas-harbingering ways of the waves threatening to rend all vessels of thoughts asunder, rest ashored. Moreover, I find the assumption that I “probably think” quite absurd. I do not (just go two sentences upstream). Neither do I think probitically (this should definitely be a word, how in the world has humanity gone on for so long without needing it?2), probabilitistically, or deterministically (that alone, I feel, would be worse than the present condition). Most prominently, I think (figurative, just an expression), the way I do not think (literal), is provably. Three dimensions suffocate as they’re surreptitiously stuffed into zero much like so many sardines in a crushd tin (possible through the misdirection masking the fact that neither the case we can’t get off of nor the point possesses anything material3).

Ahh, so an allusion requires the audacious to share in the cultural knowledge of the audience? Perfetto, remain as I must a drop of milk in a world of cheese, there goes my last dreg of author-ity. Not that there was much to begin with. Call this a patchwork then, a haphazardly stitched together product borrowing from multiple tapestries – nothing but their Persian flaws – a fleeting fluttering facsimile of a clown’s garb at the Carnaval, one’s image of the Cologne Cathedral’s windows were they camembert clocks left in the sun. More snobbishly, a Pastiche. From the Italian Pasticcio, a pâte of ingredients crushed and mushed and scrunched and bunched into a grotesquely textured glob, a vocalic subversion making you think of your possibly favourite flavour of gelato (why do I feel people will begin to suspect I’m hungry when I’m writing these?). A scratch on some past itch, a vain attempt to dull some past ache, a way to pass the ticks of the clock when the tocks on the dock leave much to be desired. In any case, the illusionary safety of an allusion is now well and truly off4. This bodes not well, least of all for my puns. So go ahead, empty a magazine into my brain (but make it NatGeo5), riddle my skull with holes in my knowledge. Secondary after-thoughts to this constitution are Chekovian, after all.

I see your point and raise you a but here’s what I6. There is a scale beyond which a conglomerate of systems can gain an animus, and not solely to symphonise expectations with apophenic epiphanies, but equally a moment post which an opus becomes an opera by the virtuous virtue of the virtues of its arias, the belt of Orion shines brighter, the piqued monocle at the Circus becomes the masked pinnacle (okay personal opinion) of the zenith of Pointillism, a Shakespeare reference smoother than you could’ve imagined smacks you in the face precisely as you like it; the infliction of inflections of affections being bidirectional. Similarly, such inflections (along with the natural corollary) exist in all things we engage with, we consume, we think we like (call this a generalised Stockholm Syndrome). A point post which every bug is a feature. A personal point of abstraction beyond which everything else is mere embellishment, a shroud adorning the real real essence of your adoration, a facet for first-timers to behold and despair for it is nothing like the wonder they were promised, but you, for one, cannot think of anything better. I, for one, cannot think of anything better than a Star Trek reference, or many things better than Star Trek (TNG) for that matter (skill issue?). Yet I am wary of recommending (especially given how good the recs I receive usually turn out to be), wary of coming across as a classycyst still imagining colossi when trunkless legs are all that remain, of having shunned realism and reality to the extent of no longer remaining relatable. But then I find solace in your argument that Escher was and perchance remains the one and only true realist (what did I say about pronouns?).

Certain realities are hard to assimilate. Like the fact that the most extroverted person you know is capable of having introversions, or any versions different from the ones that made themselves apparent at the initial acquaintance (calling them intro-versions would be too much on the nose). That one likely takes this for granted for oneself, and permits the crassest rationalisations for one’s own ineptitudes7, is clearly a dichotomy, a citric parable of the usual centric paragon wantonly claiming ceteris paribus. Assumptions of long-term support remain specious, and that, I’ll write, is a greater good (unless it’s too late to try and reclaim that term). This whole shebang of scripted (un)interpretables, in fact, has been an attempt to find a version beyond the current, lying outside the distribution of the present. An attempt to make this text make me get there. Can I? Can it? Can uncanny canons can it long enough for me to? Can I risk it with a can o’ biscuits when even cannabis (kidding, kidding) couldn’t? (I know, I know, need to start thinking about the meter on these.) When in truth the only time I’ve felt even close to being twice-cooked was while reading Rodel reading Gospodinov, and contemporaneously listening to The Strokes listening to themselves wondering whether this was, indeed, it? (It was.) When rather you’d, when much rather ut, canis a corio? (Whoops, close call. I’ll try to steer clear of nested refs hereon.) Can I cool it like Coolidge8 and resort to my retorts of syllabic frugality for that is when I am at my most ernest? Thus far, the answer’s been quite underwhelming. I simply tend to become more of myself, and even then not to an extent that would, in any sense, count. I don’t know whether this remains an actualisable possibility, I don’t know if I can let myself go that far.

(I’d say this is a good place to press Ctrl+W and forget this text exists. You’ve been warned.)

Allthis mightjust makesense iffonly wun weeds through the leyes of a lunatic.

One keeps forgetting that possession of the faculty of language does not make one intelligent, and it therefore becomes necessary to disillusion one of the former as well. I will never understand the enlightened conclusion on the superiority of the active over the passive, of the voiced presumption of agency when none demonstrably exists, of praising the earth for jigsaws falling into place; I’m clearly just pretending I got the beat. Call me Geor Geor Binks.

See? I just keep going through the same motions, and this repetitiveness has started to irk me a little. But how does one avoid going back to the same patterns again and again? A way to circumvent the problem seems apparent – and we’ll see how that goes – through anthropomorphising these patterns into an instrument practically ubiquitous; a character. For then the character is the motif in itself, getting repeated through the necessary virtue of continued existence. The rest of this text, then, shall focus on somebody else. But this being an attempt of dubious sincerity, I wouldn’t hold many expectations.

Call him Ed.

Some minutes ago – a precise quantification of which would be both redundant and contrary to the motive of this sentence – having little left to do for the day, he arrived at the beach, once more to observe his shadow lengthening over the water as he gazes towards the West, where the sun rose from today, as it usually does. Today, Ed has been diverting himself in the curiosity of periodically tracing something in the sand and watching the waves of brine salt and level his words over and over again. His personal Carthago. There is some inexplicable comfort for Ed in this satire of a situation, and his mind invariably turns to Horace (for it has quite definitively turned away from Seneca) – and then, just because there’s nothing more seemingly worthwhile to do at the moment, to splitting the notion of the poet into two, differentiated by some internally vocalised ohs and oohs – a Horatio, one with limited philosophies but a plenitude of well-rounded slumber in which to dream them; and a Horatius, making his final stand at some synaptic bridge keeping all belligerent thoughts threatening his peace from entering the Rome of his mind.

When the bridge fails to fall (chronologically) before the onslaught, when cries of struggling forces start emanating from where they clash, does he also imagine a crosshair on the same ray as the one from Rome to the point of emanations? Or does he, as he once feigned to claim, transport these pulsations below the sol, the soil, the sand10? Does he, when the coadunation of caverns caged inside him, the true source of these thumpings, refuses to be buried, to be cached underneath some wooden floor, wish for a cold hand of reason to phase through and hold it still11? (We do not know, for even in this assumed omniscience we must remain, true to our natures, indecisive.)

All this reminiscing about failed states of mind makes Ed wish to preserve his words in the sand a tad bit longer. He has an idea. Not a good one, but it will work at least once. As soon as the water withdraws, he digs a moat around his canvas. As the wine-coloured waves return, they flood the moat instead, and for once the words he’d written remain untouched - surrounded by a ring of iridescence. For a moment he fantasises about donning this ring somehow and vanishing from the world, but this is not the ring of Gyges. The ring is not, as much as he might will it through the lens of Irish prepositions, o’Gygia either. And he is not that bearer of curses of poly-famy. (He smiles. He’s not sure if he’s smiling because he found this funny or because he knows nobody else will.) It is, anyway, already too late for one to fulfil one’s quest by planting an oar in a land amidst confusion. There will be confusion, but not the kind that absolves him. Ed doesn’t really want absolution to begin with. What kind of a person deals with absolutes?

Ed realises now that his sand-ridden hands have not been as idle as his brain – one has been tousling his hair while the other percussed some untempered rhythm on his knee – and he doesn’t really mind either. The former, actually, is quite welcome, for with the exception of a periodic pogonotomy and maybe some pretence when it comes to his moustache, he doesn’t have much of a truck with aesthetics. To appear kempt, he feels, would be to claim he exists on purpose, something which could not be further from the truth. (At this particular moment, Ed is enervated enough to feel he has earned not to at all, but also knows that were he to hang over the city of his dreams, he would not be mist.) The latter? Ed finds some ineffable and sempiternal beauty in the patterns of the sand on the fabric of his trousers, or rather the potential for a beauty that may be introduced, far surpassing any that exists.

He takes some sand and watches it slip through his fingers, resolving to fossilise this notion in its original potency, but maybe just for himself. To himself, he shall apply the Rationale and the Principle he has identified to stand above all else in his solitary ventures. To others, he’ll wax eloquent through the mouths of friends (the likes of the existence of which will be a deus ex machina in itself, at the very beginning of his narrative), of sculpted swathes of green, at the same time having asserted for such dimensions a limit in consequences. Thus he shall keep proselytising for unrounded squares through nothing but quasi-laterals. He smiles again. A joke has brushed past his consciousness, but to verbalise it would be to pin it down, would be to pluck out its feathers and trim its tail. Maybe some day someone will notice the binaries in the litanies of his prosened cons. And they will, perhaps, realise in their drawn conclusions how the manies of the natural gaze at the unhatched possibilities cradled by the fews of the artifice. All the possibilities cocooned inside that cosmic egg from which, of course, omne vivum. Ed’s afraid his dreams of sating his curiosity about what lies within without the deontological burden of having cracked the shell and letting it all spill by suspending it in a vinaigrous ether must remain precisely so, meagre surrealisms until someone proves the ether real (which, of course, will not happen). Till then, he’ll resign himself to looking for discoveries in the act of chipping away at assumptions through acts of minor creations.

But he pauses that thought as the murmuring in the background crosses his threshold of comfort. Others are coming his way. As good a cue as any to leave, not only because in all his grand moments Ed has been alone (for he cannot afford himself a less impecunious notion of grandeur), but because he has for a while now failed to see the halos around the people he was focusing on, something he had grown quite used to. He would rather not greet the now-blurried faces with his hurriedly slurried salutations. For with his dilapidating vision, all he sees are façades of bildungs drained dry of all romanticism. (And whom can he blame but himself? Of late, Ed has wondered whether this arduous climb to Parnassus is worth the blood it demands, no matter how terribly mesmerising the view from the summit turns out to be.) So masks are all he sees, a sophistry ensuring that masques are all he throws. The throes of sweet bergamasques remain his to mull over under the pallor of the plaster Mentor in a lazy orbit above his shoulders. And these are the ones he must return to. He must, once again, make his eyes heavy (with tomes long forgotten) to ensure they sink deep enough into a pillow not to fly open at the rapacious tappings of a post-midnight call – he’s had enough of those already. In all fairness, Ed would value an exclamation of the Archimedean persuasion far more than one of ‘Galatea!’.

AFTERWORD

It took a long while before I had the time to realise it was past time I started thinking about writing this one. Then I took an equally long while to vainly wait to become the person I wanted this to be written by, deciding to make do with what I had enfin. The writing itself took some time. Proofreading, however, took far, far longer. At this point, I am afraid I can but leave it to you (lie? egregious if.) to decide whether a seeming irregularity qualifies as a typographical error or an orthographic decision.

Some words are new. Some are old. Others ancient, maybe. None, however, in my opinion, are obsolete. The twenty-first century ire for relevance is the impatience of Gollum knowing the answer to every single one of the hobbit’s riddles. The twenty-first century ire for obsolescence is the impatience of Gollum not knowing what the hobbit has in his pocketses. There’s the past ache, there’s the repetition of which I wrote. But I’m most likely not going to change, most likely not going to stop dreading sunny days. Keats and Yeats remain on your side.
